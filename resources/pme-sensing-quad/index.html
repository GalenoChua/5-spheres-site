The PME Sensing Quad

Train your sensing muscle. In yourself, and in others.

The PME Sensing Quad is a practice tool for training your sensing muscle. It works in pairs and takes about twenty minutes.

You use it when you want to get better at reading what is actually happening, in yourself and in the person across from you. Not interpreting, not diagnosing. Sensing. Noticing what is there before you start making stories about it.

Zone 1: The Scan

One person speaks for six minutes. The other person only senses. No advice, no fixing, no reassurance. You scan three layers:

1
Physical. Scan your body. Heart rate, tension, energy. What is it telling you that you have ignored?
2
Mental. Where is your head right now? Clear or fogged? Racing or stuck? Sharp or scattered?
3
Emotional. What is the weather in there right now? Name it as plainly as you can.

The observer's job is to reflect something back. Paraphrase, or offer a hunch as a question: "I'm sensing... is that right?"

Zone 2: The Quad

After the round, both people rate all four dimensions from 1 to 5. Be honest. Your lowest score is the point.

How well did others sense me?

How close were they to what was actually true.

How well did I sense others?

When it was their turn to be read.

How present was I?

Here and connected, or drifting and half-forming my reply.

How well did I sense myself?

In the hot seat, and how well I put what I feel and think into words.

Your lowest score is your 90-day focus

Sense others low? Train curiosity. Present low? Stop drifting, stay in it. Sense self low? Build the daily scan. Being sensed low? Let people in, close the gap.
